Volcanic Glass Sample — Exhibit Label

Facts

Material: Obsidian flake; Date: Late Holocene; Region: Sahel caravan margin.

Interpretation

The sample carries travel in its atoms—trace elements that map flows of glass from distant vents to river towns. Edge polish hints at brief use and frequent handling; a teaching piece as much as a tool.

On display, courtesy of: Field School Depot.

Obsidian Spearhead — Collection Note

Material: Volcanic glass (obsidian). Function: Spear point / hunting & defense.

Location Found: Dune-margin campsite, inland Sahel.

Obsidian is sharper than steel but brittle. Parallel pressure flakes show controlled manufacture and a slim, true-flying profile. Haft polish indicates fiber wrap and resin; tip step-fractures hint at contact with bone or dense hide. Sources are non-local, mapping mobility and trade.

Coprolite Sample — Collection Note

Material: Fossilized dung. Function: Dietary & environmental record.

Location Found: Shelter floor lens.

Coprolites unravel into starches, bone splinters, phytoliths, and parasite eggs—evidence for meals, seasons, and health. This sample holds milled grass starch and fish-scale microplates, pointing to a riverine diet.

Basaltic Glyphstone — Collection Note

Material: Basalt with iron-rich patina. Function: Boundary / oath marker.

Location Found: River terrace.

Shallow crossing incisions serve as witnessed promises. Non-local basalt anchors law to deep time; grooves sometimes held rubbed pigment for counting at dawn.

Bronze Kohl Container — Collection Note

Material: Cast bronze; traces of galena.

Function: Cosmetic storage.

Kohl is both beauty and medicine; lining eyelids cuts glare and may slow infection. Darkened lip shows years of use; patina is devotion turned metal.

Cranial Vault Fragment — Collection Note

Material: Human bone.

Function: Osteological evidence.

Inner vascular impressions and a healed linear groove suggest an adult who survived injury. Ethical handling is paramount: this is evidence and a life.

Faience Scarab Amulet — Collection Note

Material: Silica glaze with copper colorants.

Function: Protective amulet.

Blue-green faience promises water and life in a dry land. The drill hole is cord-polished; the underside bears a simple cross-hatched motif tuned to local taste.
