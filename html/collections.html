Mask of the Harmattan — Exhibit Label

Facts

Material: Carved iroko wood; fiber fringe; mineral pigment. Approx. date: 17–19th c. (oral chronologies). Region: Inland Sahel trade corridor. Dimensions: 36 × 22 × 14 cm. On display courtesy of the Diallo family archive.

Interpretation

The mask is associated with the season when the dry Harmattan wind arrives, blanching sky and skin alike. Its slit eyes and jutting jaw exaggerate scarcity: breath is short, water is counted, words are measured…

Comparative Notes

Comparable pigment mixtures and fiber techniques are found along the trans-Sahel corridor.

Cautionary Note on Interpretation

Attribution of exact clan use is debated; avoid definitive claims without community input.

On display, courtesy of: The Diallo family archive.
