Museum Staff Profiles

Dr. Amina Selim Archaeologist

Chief Curator & Field Director

Expertise: funerary architecture, ceramic typology, LIDAR/photogrammetry.

Dr. Selim earned her BA at Cairo University and a PhD at the University of Chicago focusing on funerary landscapes. She directs drone and LIDAR surveys to map eroded foundations and is known for meticulous ceramic reconstruction and pigment analysis. Her tool belt mixes field notebooks, a total station, and open-source GIS. Colleagues describe her as precise and relentlessly curious. She publishes in open journals and trains local students in non-destructive methods.

Museum role: Lead curator for River to Sand; field director for the Sahara Corridor Project.

Special: Arabic/English; repatriation & community co-curation; open dig diaries.

Dr. Luis Ortega Physical Anthropologist

Objects Conservator

Expertise: osteology, trauma/stress markers, isotopic diet analysis; micro-CT.

Dr. Ortega (UNAM; UCLA) studies how migration, diet, and labor leave traces on bone. He pairs micro-CT with stable-isotope sampling to reconstruct life histories ethically. A careful cataloger and thoughtful mentor, he foregrounds descendant consultation and clear public communication.

Museum role: Human Remains Stewardship Lead; repatriation liaison; co-curator of Bodies of Evidence.

Special: Spanish/English bilingual; caption-first lab practices; plain-language labels.

Prof. Mei-Lin Zhao Interpreter

Registrar

Discipline: Mythography & Cultural Semiotics · Lens: Decolonial Museology & Indigenous Storywork.

Zhao holds an MA in Folklore (Taipei) and a DPhil in Museum Studies (Oxford). She collaborates with linguists, weavers, and ritual artists to build exhibitions where objects speak through pattern and gesture. A generous skeptic, she challenges spectacle while protecting wonder.

Method: Cross-reference oral histories/archives; compare motifs; prototype immersive labels; carefully documented AI ritual modeling.

Special: Shared-authority labels; teaches “holding uncertainty”; community review can overturn her drafts—and she welcomes it.

Riley Thompson Community Storyweaver

Learning & Public Programs

Expertise: Data-viz for heritage, accessible interaction design, WebGL maps.

Designer-developer who turns field data into interactive maps and captioned media. Partners with curators to ensure high-contrast palettes, transcripts, and screen-reader-friendly labels. Builds bridges between research, stories, and visitors.

Method: Co-design with artists; builds alt-text libraries; A/B tests readability.

Special: First-gen college grad; open-source advocate.
